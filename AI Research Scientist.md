## 1. What does an AI Research Scientist do?

### What the interviewer is evaluating
Whether you understand this role as **research-driven**, not product or platform engineering.

### What you should learn
- Core AI research areas  
- Difference between research and applied roles  
- Scientific contribution workflows  

### How to answer
Frame the role around advancing knowledge and methods.

### Sample Answer
An AI Research Scientist focuses on advancing the state of the art in AI by developing new models, algorithms, or theoretical insights. The role emphasizes experimentation, rigorous evaluation, and publishing or transferring research findings into applied settings.

---

## 2. How is an AI Research Scientist different from an Applied AI Research Engineer?

### What the interviewer is evaluating
Role clarity.

### What you should learn
- Research vs production focus  
- Evaluation depth  

### How to answer
Contrast discovery with translation.

### Sample Answer
An AI Research Scientist focuses on novel ideas and theoretical or empirical breakthroughs, while an Applied AI Research Engineer focuses on adapting existing research into production-ready systems.

---

## 3. How do you choose a research problem?

### What the interviewer is evaluating
Research judgment.

### What you should learn
- Problem formulation  
- Impact assessment  

### How to answer
Explain motivation and feasibility.

### Sample Answer
I choose problems based on potential impact, novelty, feasibility, and alignment with available data and compute. I also consider whether the problem advances understanding or unlocks new capabilities.

---

## 4. How do you evaluate whether a research idea is novel?

### What the interviewer is evaluating
Literature awareness.

### What you should learn
- Literature review techniques  
- Benchmark awareness  

### How to answer
Reference systematic exploration.

### Sample Answer
I conduct a thorough literature review, analyze existing benchmarks, and compare assumptions and results to ensure the idea meaningfully differs from prior work.

---

## 5. How do you design experiments for AI research?

### What the interviewer is evaluating
Scientific rigor.

### What you should learn
- Controlled experiments  
- Reproducibility  

### How to answer
Emphasize hypothesis-driven design.

### Sample Answer
I start with a clear hypothesis, control variables carefully, run ablations, and use statistically meaningful comparisons to validate conclusions.

---

## 6. What metrics do you use to evaluate models?

### What the interviewer is evaluating
Evaluation maturity.

### What you should learn
- Task-specific metrics  
- Limitations of benchmarks  

### How to answer
Discuss metric selection.

### Sample Answer
I select metrics that align with the research objective, such as accuracy, perplexity, or task-specific scores, while also acknowledging their limitations and complementing them with qualitative analysis.

---

## 7. How do you ensure reproducibility in research?

### What the interviewer is evaluating
Research integrity.

### What you should learn
- Code and data versioning  
- Experiment tracking  

### How to answer
Stress documentation and rigor.

### Sample Answer
I ensure reproducibility by versioning code and data, logging experiment configurations, fixing random seeds where possible, and clearly documenting methods and assumptions.

---

## 8. How do you handle negative or inconclusive results?

### What the interviewer is evaluating
Research maturity.

### What you should learn
- Scientific honesty  
- Iterative research  

### How to answer

### Sample Answer

### What the interviewer is evaluating

### What you should learn
