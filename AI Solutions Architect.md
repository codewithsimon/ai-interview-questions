## 1. What does an AI Solutions Architect do?

### What the interviewer is evaluating
Whether you understand this role as **customer-facing system design**, not hands-on feature coding only.

### What you should learn
- Solution architecture  
- Customer requirements translation  
- AI system tradeoffs  

### How to answer
Frame the role around designing **end-to-end AI solutions**.

### Sample Answer
An AI Solutions Architect designs end-to-end AI systems that solve business problems. The role focuses on translating customer requirements into scalable, secure, and cost-effective AI architectures while balancing technical and business constraints.

---

## 2. How is an AI Solutions Architect different from an AI Platform Engineer?

### What the interviewer is evaluating
Role clarity.

### What you should learn
- Internal vs external focus  
- Responsibility boundaries  

### How to answer
Contrast customer solutions with internal platforms.

### Sample Answer
An AI Platform Engineer builds internal infrastructure for teams, while an AI Solutions Architect designs customer-facing solutions using existing platforms and services to meet specific business needs.

---

## 3. How do you gather requirements for an AI solution?

### What the interviewer is evaluating
Discovery and communication skills.

### What you should learn
- Requirement elicitation  
- Business goal alignment  

### How to answer
Start from business outcomes.

### Sample Answer
I begin by understanding the business problem, success metrics, constraints, and risk tolerance. I then translate these into technical requirements and validate assumptions with stakeholders.

---

## 4. How do you decide whether a problem should use AI or traditional software?

### What the interviewer is evaluating
Architectural judgment.

### What you should learn
- AI suitability criteria  
- Cost and risk analysis  

### How to answer
Focus on value and feasibility.

### Sample Answer
I recommend AI when the problem involves unstructured data, ambiguity, or pattern recognition that rules-based systems cannot handle reliably. Otherwise, traditional software is often simpler and safer.

### What the interviewer is evaluating
System design ability.

### What you should learn
- LLM pipelines  
- Retrieval  
- Integration patterns

### What the interviewer is evaluating
Growth planning.

### What you should learn
- Load patterns  
- Scaling strategies

### Sample Answer
